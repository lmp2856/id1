Exercise 7: Website Analysis

96 Elephants

  1. What adjectives would you use to describe the design of this site?

    Bold

  2. Describe how the navigation for this “story” works.

    The navigation menu is one centered column.

    On a chapter page, what is the visual cue for you to go to the next chapter.

    There is a block with text indicating the next story. The block also changes color when hovering over it.

    Do you ever feel lost while browsing this story? Why or why not?

    I do not feel lost while navigating because the story leads the user into clicking the next link.

  3. How does the ending for Chapter 3 differ from the endings for the other 2 chapters?

    The ending for the third chapter is open-ended; meaning, that YOU, the user, can help create the ending to the story of the current elephant genocide.

  4. What do you think the creators of this site hoped to accomplish?

    Their intention is to educate the masses on the destructive genocide of elephants and to involve the user into taking action.

Elizabeth River Trail

  1. What adjectives would you use to describe the design of this site?

    Visual, neat & clean

  2. Describe how the navigation works.

    There are three links in the upper right hand corner of the page.

    What pages are in the navigation?

    Explore, History, and Future.

    Do you ever feel lost while browsing the site? Why or why not?

    No, because the navigation bar is constantly at the top of the page.

  3. Do the page names in the navigation seem appropriate for the content on these pages?

    Yes, every title is directly associated with the content it provides.

  4. What do you think the creators of this site hoped to accomplish?

    They want to get people out to the trail so that they can experience it, in hopes that the user's in-person experience will prompt them to help volunteer or donate.
