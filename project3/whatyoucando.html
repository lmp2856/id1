What Can I Do To End Factory Farming?

  • One easy way to combat factory farming is to stop consuming animal products. Adopting a vegan lifestyle may seem impossible, but it is easier to do so today. With the aid of the Internet, you can find hundreds of delicious vegan recipes, as well as various information on the vegan lifestyle. If you are hesitant to become vegan, start off by becoming vegetarian. Giving up meat is a healthy first step. Once you feel adjusted, you can then transition yourself into giving up dairy and other animal products.

    Here are some helpful links to help you learn about and adopt a vegan lifestyle:

    • VeganSociety.com - How To Go Vegan
    • VeganKit.com - Becoming Vegan

  • If you are still adamant on eating meat, you can still begin to help end factory farming. As a consumer, you can make food choices that help put a dent in the high demand for animal products. You can do this by:

    • Buying fewer animal products
    • Limiting your meat in take to only a couple of times a week, instead of almost every meal
    • Refusing to buy factory farmed products from supermarkets and restaurants
    • Start asking retailers where their animal products come from
    • Buying only certified humane and eco-friendly brands that you have extensively researched
    • Educating your peers on the practices and effects of factory farming

  • Here are some links to help you make more conscious shopping decisions:

    • FarmForward.com - Food Choices
    • FarmForward.com - Animal Welfare Certifications
    • BuyingPoultry.com (This site will help narrow your search for brands and retailers that support high-welfare poultry and plant-based products)
